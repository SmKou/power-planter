Power Planter

Tend to three plants, keeping track of their stats as they grow. Feed them, hydrate them, and give them light to maintain their ideal levels, and raise them to reveal their colors, patterns and and the colors of those patterns, and petal shapes, all of which affect a plant's capacity to grow. Should any of a plant's stats reach 0 or exceed the maximum, they die, and if left in wilting condition for too long, a plant will die.

Do your best to keep your plants as well as okay. If their condition is poor, they will only become more and more eratic as they grow .

Type: Numbers game

What Plant is it?

Color

The primary color of a plant reflects its relative ideal amounts of different nutrients. Its ideal can fall into one of three categories: low, mid, and high. While it's recommended to keep their levels within a short range of their ideals, plants can differ in their tolerance.

  • Red prefers a mid level of soil, high level of water, and low level of light.
  • Yellow prefers a low level of soil, mid level of water, and high level of light.
  • Blue prefers a high level of soil, low level of water, and mid level of light.
Pattern

The pattern of a plant reflects how much nutrients it tends to absorb when fed, hydrated or synthesized. While this amount deviates by nutrient, there is a given range for the pattern that the amount will fall in. Be aware that this range is increased with each state of growth.

  • Plain tends to absorb little nutrients.
  • Spotted tends to absorb some nutrients.
  • Striped tends to absorb a lot of nutrients.
Pattern Color

The color of a plant's pattern reflects how much of its nutrients it uses up every few seconds. Like its pattern, the amount deviates by nutrient and the range increases with each state of growth.

  • Red tends to use a some nutrients for energy.
  • Yellow tends to use a lot of nutrients for energy.
  • Blue tends to use a little nutrients for energy.
Petal shape

The shape of a plant's flower petals reflects its tolerance, meaning how the difference between its ideal levels and current levels affect its condition. This will not be confirmed until a plant's final stage of growth, when it flowers.

  • Triangular petals have a low tolerance.
  • Circular petals have a mid tolerance.
  • Diamond petals have a high tolerance.
